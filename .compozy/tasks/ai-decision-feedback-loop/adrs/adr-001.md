# ADR-001: Store AI decision feedback on the ticket

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-04

## Context

The product now shows AI governance recommendations, but operators still cannot record whether an AI decision was useful, needed review, or was incorrect. Human approval gates exist for high-risk decisions, yet approval is not the same as quality feedback. The feedback needs to stay close to the ticket context, actor, audit trail, and agent decision.

## Decision

Store structured AI decision feedback directly on `ticket.ai.feedback` and expose a scoped ticket mutation endpoint. The first version supports feedback for triage and resolution draft decisions with a rating, optional note, actor, timestamp, and audit entry.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Global feedback repository

- **Description**: Create a separate repository and aggregate feedback independent of tickets.
- **Pros**: Easier analytics later.
- **Cons**: Adds persistence and join complexity before the workflow is validated.
- **Why rejected**: Ticket-local feedback is enough for the first operational loop.

### Alternative 2: Free-form follow-up only

- **Description**: Ask analysts to write feedback as internal comments.
- **Pros**: No data model change.
- **Cons**: Feedback is hard to query, score, or use in future evals.
- **Why rejected**: The goal is a repeatable improvement loop, not unstructured comments.

## Consequences

### Positive

- AI decision quality becomes visible and auditable per ticket.
- Future evals can consume structured feedback.
- The implementation stays within existing ticket authorization and persistence patterns.

### Negative

- Feedback analytics remain basic until a later aggregation cycle.
- Ticket payloads grow slightly.

### Risks

- Low-quality feedback could pollute future evals. Mitigation: capture actor and note, and restrict mutation to ticket workers.

## Implementation Notes

Add domain types, orchestrator mutation, HTTP route, frontend API, UI controls in the AI decision card, CSS, backend integration tests, and e2e coverage.
